The usual way to add a feature to hardware that's already installed in
someone's basement is to sell them new hardware. Enphase Energy took a
different route this week: a software update to its IQ Gateway that gives
owners of its second-generation IQ Battery 3T and 10T systems something
they didn't have — backup power during an outage — without pulling out
anything they already own
([GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/07/21/3330403/20176/en/Enphase-Energy-Unlocks-Backup-and-Expandable-Storage-for-Existing-IQ-Battery-Customers-Across-Europe.html)).

Until now, those older batteries worked only in grid-tied mode: fine for
shifting solar power around the day, useless the moment the grid went down.
The update, rolling out across Germany, the Netherlands, France, Belgium,
Sweden, Switzerland, Luxembourg, Austria, Spain, Denmark, Portugal, Greece,
and Finland, changes that — and it lets owners mix battery generations in
one system, adding a new IQ System Controller or IQ Battery 5P alongside
their existing units rather than replacing them outright as their power
needs grow with a heat pump or an EV in the garage.

"Our customers can now combine batteries from different generations, add
backup, and expand capacity without abandoning their original investment,"
said Enphase's Sabbas Daniel. It's a small announcement next to a rocket
launch, but it's the same underlying idea as the satellites above: treating
expensive hardware that still works as worth extending, not replacing.
