---
title: A retired coal mine gets a second life as a solar and pumped-hydro plant
kicker: Climate
author: The Editors
summary: New South Wales approved a $1.8 billion project that will turn a former coal site into 12 hours of long-duration energy storage for up to 120,000 homes.

---

The Stratford Mining Complex, about 100 kilometers north of Newcastle,
Australia, spent decades producing coal until operations there ended in
2024. On July 15, the New South Wales government granted final planning
approval to turn the site into something else entirely: a 300-megawatt
pumped hydro plant with 12 hours of storage, paired with a 320-megawatt
solar farm
([NSW Government](https://www.nsw.gov.au/ministerial-releases/mine-site-becomes-renewable-energy-generator);
corroborated by [Energy-Storage.News](https://www.energy-storage.news/australias-new-south-wales-grants-planning-consent-for-3-6gwh-pumped-hydro-project-at-former-coal-mine/)
and [RenewEconomy](https://reneweconomy.com.au/coal-giant-gets-nod-to-convert-retired-mine-into-solar-and-pumped-hydro-powerhouse-with-12-hours-storage/)).

The $1.8 billion project, developed by Gloucester Coal Pty Ltd — a
subsidiary of Yancoal Australia — will use a tunnel to move water between
a new upper reservoir and an existing dam on the site, storing surplus
daytime solar power and releasing it during evening demand peaks. It's the
first pumped hydro project to win full planning approval in New South
Wales in six years, and it's expected to supply about 13% of the state's
2034 long-duration storage target on its own.

"We are taking a former mine site and giving it a new purpose — powering
up to 120,000 homes across New South Wales," said NSW Minister for Energy
Yasmin Catley.

The project is also expected to create up to 350 construction jobs and
ten ongoing positions, with a voluntary agreement to direct $18.2 million
to the local Mid Coast Council over its lifetime. There's a certain
symmetry to a coal mine becoming a battery for the grid that's replacing
it — infrastructure that once pulled energy out of the ground now storing
it instead.
