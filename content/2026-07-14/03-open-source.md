---
title: Wikipedia's new tone-check tool made newcomer edits stick, so it's live for everyone
kicker: Open Source
author: The Editors
summary: A guided task that flags "peacock" language lifted new-editor task completion by nearly 39% with no drop in edit quality — it's now on by default across four language Wikipedias.
order: 3
---

Getting a first-time Wikipedia editor to actually finish an edit is harder
than it sounds — most suggested tasks lose people partway through. The
Wikimedia Foundation's Growth team has been testing a fix: a structured
task called Revise Tone, which points newcomers to sentences likely to
violate the site's neutral-point-of-view policy — the kind of overly
promotional "peacock" language editors have long flagged by hand — and
explains the relevant guideline, without dictating a fix. The judgment call
stays with the human.

Run head-to-head against the default "Copyedit" suggested task on the
Arabic, English, French, and Portuguese Wikipedias, Revise Tone lifted
newcomer task-completion rates by 38.7%, with no measured decrease in edit
quality. The test wrapped up on July 9, and this week the Foundation
switched it on by default for everyone on those four wikis, configurable
per-community through Community Configuration, with more languages planned
([Wikimedia Diff, Tech News 2026 Issue 29](https://diff.wikimedia.org/2026/07/13/tech-news-2026-issue-29/); [technical writeup on MediaWiki.org](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Growth/Revise_Tone)).

It's a small feature by the standards of a project that runs on volunteer
labor at planetary scale — but it's the kind of unglamorous, quietly
tested improvement that compounds. A tool that turns a few more curious
first-time visitors into editors who actually finish their first edit is,
multiplied across millions of pageviews, exactly how an encyclopedia
maintained entirely by strangers keeps getting better.
