---
title: An AI recipe made display pixels last 40 times longer
kicker: Materials
author: The Editors
summary: Seoul National University researchers trained a model to design solvent blends for quantum-dot LEDs, doubling efficiency and multiplying operating lifetime.
order: 5
---

Quantum-dot LEDs promise sharper, more efficient displays, but getting
the microscopic dots to pack evenly into a working film has long been
a matter of trial and error with solvents — slow, and hard to improve
on by hand. A team led by professors Jeonghun Kwak at Seoul National
University and Jaehoon Lim at Sungkyunkwan University handed the
problem to a machine learning model instead.

The model was trained on solvent properties — vapor pressure,
viscosity, density, dielectric constant — paired with measurements of
how evenly quantum-dot films actually packed, taken by atomic force
microscopy. Rather than testing solvents one at a time, it worked
backward: predicting what an ideal solvent's properties should look
like, then letting researchers blend real solvents to match. Devices
built with the AI-designed blends reached roughly double the efficiency
of ones made with a single conventional solvent, and lasted more than
40 times longer before degrading. The work, led by Beomsoo Chun,
published July 15 in *Reports on Progress in Physics*
([phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2026-07-ai-qled-recipe-efficiency-boosts.html)).

It's a small, unglamorous kind of AI story — no chatbot, no headline
demo — just a search space too large for humans to explore by hand,
handed to a model built to explore it. That's often what "quietly got
better" looks like from the inside.
