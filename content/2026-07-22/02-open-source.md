---
title: Firefox ships JPEG XL support and picks up the pace
kicker: Open Source
author: The Editors
summary: Firefox 153 lands experimental support for a better image format and PDF merging, and Mozilla says future releases will come twice as often.
order: 2
---

Firefox 153 shipped Tuesday, and buried in the changelog is a small bet on
an image format that has struggled to get anywhere: JPEG XL, now available
experimentally through the Firefox Labs panel in Settings
([Mozilla release notes](https://www.firefox.com/en-US/firefox/153.0/releasenotes/);
corroborated by
[AlternativeTo](https://alternativeto.net/news/2026/7/firefox-153-adds-containers-hdr-video-on-windows-pdf-merging-and-jpeg-xl-image-support/)
and [Phoronix](https://www.phoronix.com/news/Firefox-153-Downloads)). JPEG
XL compresses better than JPEG, PNG, WebP, and GIF alike, and was designed
to eventually replace all four — a genuinely useful piece of open
infrastructure that browser vendors have mostly ignored since Google
dropped support for it in Chrome in 2022. Firefox turning it back on,
even behind a flag, is a vote that the format isn't dead.

The rest of the release is smaller but useful: HDR video playback on
Windows, the ability to merge PDFs by dragging files into the sidebar, a
right-click option to generate a QR code for any open tab, and Containers —
a feature for keeping logins and cookies for different accounts cleanly
separated — arriving out of its long incubation in Firefox's experimental
builds.

Mozilla also confirmed a bigger shift: starting in September, Firefox
moves from a four-week release cycle to two weeks, echoing a change Chrome
made in 2024. "Work that is not ready should not be rushed, and features
can still take the time they need to bake," said Sylvestre Ledru, Mozilla's
director of engineering, framing the faster cadence as smaller, safer
releases rather than a rush to ship
([The Register](https://www.theregister.com/software/2026/07/17/mozilla-speeds-firefox-release-schedule-to-biweekly/5274423)).
Firefox 153 itself becomes the next Extended Support Release, with security
updates guaranteed into late 2027 for anyone who'd rather not keep up with
the new pace at all.
