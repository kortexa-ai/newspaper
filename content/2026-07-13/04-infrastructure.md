On Saturday, the Debian project released version 13.6 — a point
release carrying 120 security fixes and 124 stability updates for
the operating system that, mostly invisibly, underpins a vast share
of the world's servers, and by descent (through Ubuntu and dozens of
other derivatives) a great deal more.

Nothing in the release notes will trend. That is exactly what makes
it worth a paragraph in a paper about progress: hundreds of
maintainers, most of them volunteers, spent their weeks finding,
patching, reviewing, and shipping fixes so that millions of machines
they will never see keep working for people they will never meet.
The same weekly rhythm delivered fresh updates across the ecosystem
— KDE Plasma 6.6.6, Wireshark 4.6.7, PipeWire 1.6.8 — each one a
small act of maintenance in public.

Software infrastructure is one of the few utilities maintained
substantially by gift. It held up again this week.
