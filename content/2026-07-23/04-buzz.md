Jack Dorsey's Block — the company behind Square, Cash App, and Tidal —
released Buzz this week, a group chat platform that folds team messaging,
AI agents, and Git hosting into one app, published under the Apache 2.0
license with the full source on GitHub
([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/21/jack-dorsey-is-taking-on-slack-with-buzz-a-group-chat-platform-for-teams-and-their-ai-agents/);
corroborated by
[Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/374026/jack-dorseys-block-launches-buzz-a-nostr-based-slack-and-github-rival-for-ai-agents)
and [Yahoo Tech](https://tech.yahoo.com/ai/meta-ai/articles/jack-dorseys-block-launches-buzz-110343370.html)).

The pitch is straightforward: instead of switching between a chat app, a
code host, and whatever tool wraps your AI agents, Buzz puts channels,
threads, direct messages, code repositories, and agents in the same
window, with the agents present as participants in the conversation
rather than a separate panel bolted on. Dorsey described it on X as
"model-agnostic, decentralized, self-sovereign, and open source" — a
group chat "for teams of people and agents of all sizes."

Teams can self-host the whole thing or use Block's managed version, and
because the code is public at github.com/block/buzz, anyone can audit it,
fork it, or build features Block never planned. That's a meaningfully
different bet than the closed, single-vendor tools it's aimed at
replacing: no migration lock-in, no dependence on one company's roadmap
for whether AI agents get first-class treatment in your team's workflow.

It's early. The release lands as version 0.4.21, with desktop builds for
macOS, Windows, and Linux; mobile clients aren't finished yet, and even
Dorsey's own framing acknowledges it's not yet a drop-in replacement for
teams with Slack and GitHub baked into their daily habits. But an
established founder putting real engineering behind an open, self-hostable
alternative — rather than another closed platform — is worth noting on
its own, regardless of whether Buzz specifically is the one that sticks.
