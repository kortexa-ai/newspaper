---
title: Orion will phone home with lasers on its next trip around the Moon
kicker: Space
author: The Editors
summary: NASA is mounting SpaceX's Starlink laser terminals on the Orion capsule so Artemis III can beam back 4K video of astronauts practicing lunar docking.
order: 2
---

NASA announced on July 16 that it has selected SpaceX to add laser
communications to the Orion spacecraft for Artemis III, the mission
currently slated to test rendezvous and docking between Orion and
prototype commercial lunar landers ahead of a crewed Moon landing planned
for 2028
([NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/07/16/nasa-taps-spacexs-starlink-to-deliver-artemis-iii-imagery-from-orion/)).

Two Starlink "mini" laser terminals will be mounted on the outside of
Orion, built on the same crosslink technology that already connects more
than 25,000 Starlink satellites to one another in orbit. Laser, or
optical, communications encode data in infrared light rather than radio
waves, which lets a spacecraft push down far more data per second than
conventional systems allow — enough, in this case, for real-time 4K video
and imagery to reach Mission Control at NASA's Johnson Space Center as
four astronauts test docking maneuvers with the landers that will
eventually carry crew to the lunar surface.

It's a proven idea, not a first attempt: NASA validated a similar laser
link during Artemis II, successfully streaming high-definition video,
flight data, and voice communications from Orion back to Earth over
optical signals. Artemis III's version builds directly on that
demonstration, and on NASA's broader push to hand off near-Earth
communications relay work to commercial providers rather than building
and operating it all in-house.

The payoff, if the demonstration works as planned, is less abstract than
it sounds: a front-row seat, in near-real time, for one of the more
delicate parts of returning humans to the Moon — watching a spacecraft
find and dock with another one, hundreds of thousands of kilometers from
anyone who could reach it if something went wrong.
